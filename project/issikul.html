Issyk-Kul

Issyk-Kul is the largest lake in Kyrgyzstan and the second largest mountain lake in the world. It is located in the northeastern part of the country, at an altitude of 1,607 meters. The lake is 182 kilometers long and up to 60 kilometers wide, with a total area of 6,236 square kilometers.

Issyk-Kul is a popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful scenery and mild climate. The lake is surrounded by snow-capped mountains, and its shores are dotted with beaches, resorts, and campgrounds. The lake is also home to a number of hot springs, which are believed to have healing properties.

Some of the popular activities at Issyk-Kul include swimming, boating, fishing, and hiking. The lake is also a popular spot for birdwatching, with a variety of migratory birds passing through the area. In the winter, the lake is a popular destination for ice fishing and ice skating.

Issyk-Kul has a rich history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Bronze Age. The lake was an important stop on the Silk Road, and was a center of trade and culture for many centuries. Today, the lake is home to a number of historical and cultural sites, including the ancient city of Balasagun, the Burana Tower, and the Rukh Ordo Cultural Center.

Karakol Ski Resort

The resort has a modern lift system, including chairlifts, surface lifts, and a gondola. The resort also has a ski school, rental shops, and a repair shop.

The resort has a variety of amenities, including restaurants, cafes, and bars. It also has a hotel, a hostel, and a number of guesthouses. The resort is open from December to April, depending on the snow conditions.
